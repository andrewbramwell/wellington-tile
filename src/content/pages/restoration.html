Methods of Tile and Grout Cleaning

Different tiles and varying levels of dirt accumulation require specific cleaning techniques. Our tile cleaning experts consider the material, grout type, dirt level, and any potential damage to the tiles. Given these factors, they may employ one of the following methods:

Buffer Method

A method using a machine known as a buffer. After applying a cleaning solution, the buffer works across the tile floor. This water-intensive technique concludes with a rinse, or in some cases, with a water extraction machine for reduced dampness.

Jet Cleaning Method

A method where rotating water jets expel dirt. Suitable for most tile floors, many professional machines come equipped with a vacuum for the water to prevent messes.

Steam Cleaning

A widely adopted method that uses steam to weaken dirt's bond to tiles. The heat also sanitises by killing bacteria and viruses. Many steam cleaners incorporate a vacuum system for water removal.

Vacuum Cleaning

Unlike domestic vacuum cleaners, these high-power machines use a hood to create a vacuum, effectively extracting deep-seated dirt. Particularly effective for grout.

Benefits of Tile and Grout Cleaning

  1. Protection Against Diseases: Unclean surfaces can become breeding grounds for disease-causing bacteria and fungi. Regular tile and grout cleaning helps reduce the risk of infections and illnesses in the household.
  2. Allergen Reduction: Dust, pollen, and other allergens often settle on floor surfaces. Without regular cleaning, these allergens can become airborne when disturbed, leading to allergic reactions and respiratory issues. A thorough cleaning minimises these potential health risks.
  3. Visual Appeal: A home with gleaming tiles exudes cleanliness and order, making it an inviting space for residents and guests alike. In contrast, a house with grimy tiles can detract from its overall beauty and leave a negative impression on visitors.
  4. Improved Ambiance: Clean tiles and grout can uplift the mood and energy of a space. The freshness it brings can enhance the overall ambiance, making the home feel more comfortable and serene.
  5. Grout Protection: Over time, grout can absorb moisture and become a magnet for dirt and grime. Certain minerals present in the dirt can react with the grout, causing it to weaken. Regular cleaning prevents this deterioration, ensuring that the grout remains intact and the tiles stay in place.
  6. Preservation of Tile Lustre: Regular cleaning not only removes dirt but also preserves the natural shine and finish of the tiles. This means less frequent need for professional polishing or restoration and a longer lifespan for your flooring.
